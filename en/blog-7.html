Pictory AI Credits (2026): rate card, packs, and how the calculation works

This guide explains what AI Credits are for, how they are consumed (images/video/avatars), and how to control costs.

Published on April 9, 2026 Updated on April 9, 2026 ⏱️ 11 min read

TL;DR: the essentials in 30 seconds

  • AI Credits are an internal currency for generative features.
  • Each model consumes a different number of credits (image, video, avatar).
  • Monthly reset: your quota refreshes and unused credits do not roll over.
  • Add-on packs are available if you need more credits (monthly recurring).

Official source: Understanding AI Credits (Pictory Help Center) (checked on April 9, 2026).

What are AI Credits used for?

According to Pictory documentation, AI Credits act as a single pool that simplifies access to generative AI features. The goal is to avoid a messy set of separate add-ons: you spend credits to generate assets (images, video, avatars) inside the product.

Important: ElevenLabs minutes are tracked as a separate quota (AI Credits do not replace them). For plan selection, see: Pictory pricing 2026.

Consumption rate card (examples): images, video, avatars

The numbers below come from the official rate card (they may change). The point to remember: consumption depends on the model.

Images (1920 × 1080)

  • Flux Schnell: 0.6 credit / image
  • Amazon Titan G1: 0.7 credit / image
  • ByteDance Seedream: 2 credits / image
  • Google Nano Banana: 4 credits / image
  • Amazon Nova Canvas: 4 credits / image

Video generation

  • Pixverse (720p): 1.6 credits / generated second
  • Veo 3.1 Fast (1080p): 10 credits / generated second
  • Veo 3.1 (1080p): 20 credits / generated second

Avatars

  • Stock Avatar: 15 credits / 30 seconds (credit based on total avatar video duration)
  • Custom Avatar: marked as “coming soon” in the documentation

Monthly reset: what to plan for

AI Credits (like video minutes) are designed as monthly quotas: reset each billing cycle, with no rollover. If you produce in batches (10 videos at once), schedule generations in the right month so you do not hit a wall mid-project.

Tip: always validate direction with a low-cost model first, then upgrade quality only if the business impact justifies it.

Team plans: pooled credits

For Team plans, the documentation states AI Credits can be pooled at the workspace level. The team consumes a shared reserve (useful when one member generates a lot, or when you split roles between production and review).

Process tip: assign one person to track AI Credits (reporting + internal rules) if you produce at high volume.

Add-on packs (monthly): how much do they cost?

If you run out of credits, you can purchase recurring monthly add-ons. The documentation lists tiers such as:

  • 500 Extra Credits: $25/month
  • 1000 Extra Credits: $49/month
  • 1500 Extra Credits: $70/month
  • 3000 Extra Credits: $135/month
  • 5000 Extra Credits: $199/month

For clean budgeting: estimate average monthly need, then keep 15–20% headroom for campaign peaks.

Best practices: avoid burning credits

1) Prototype cheaply

Validate style and direction with a low-consumption model, then scale quality only if it pays back.

2) Standardize prompts

House prompts reduce iterations (therefore credits) and keep your videos consistent.

3) Generate only what is missing

Use stock media for 80–90% of scenes and generative AI for the hard parts (abstract concepts, transitions).

4) Track weekly

A lightweight weekly check prevents end-of-month surprises, especially for teams.

FAQ

Can free users buy add-on packs?

The documentation indicates credit add-ons are available only for paying subscriptions (not for free accounts).

Do AI Credits cover everything in Pictory?

No. Several features are listed as included without AI Credits consumption (examples: PPT extraction, Smart Templates, transcription, Copilot).

Do AI Credits replace ElevenLabs minutes?

No. ElevenLabs minutes remain a separate quota. AI Credits are for generative features (images/video/avatars).

Which plan should I pick to get more credits?

Start by estimating your consumption. Then compare “upgrade plan” versus “add-on pack” based on your monthly baseline.

Conclusion: treat AI Credits like a media budget

Plan, prototype, standardize, and use generative features only where they truly add value (retention, conversion, credibility). With a simple process, you can move faster without losing cost control.
